Officer Training Guide
Internal training documentation for use by Training Officers, Lieutenants, and above.
New Officer Training Checklist
Staff Use Only
Clearance
All items below must be completed and signed off before a Cadet is cleared for independent patrol. Clearance must be granted by a Lieutenant or above within the officer's respective agency.
Orientation
Server overview and hybrid roleplay model explanation
Chain of command introduction — know your agency's rank structure and COC
Radio use — 10-codes optional at first, encouraged as they progress
Procedures
Traffic stop procedure — full walkthrough with a training officer
Felony stop procedure — including VC command delivery format and cover positioning
Pursuit policy — staff permission, priority timer, PIT and spike rules
Arrest procedures — probable cause, Miranda rights, jail-only booking
Equipment & Appearance
Vehicle access and whitelist restrictions for their agency
Uniform classes and assignment rules — verify correct class for duty type
Final Assessment
Scenario-based test or supervised ride-along with a Corporal or above — signed off by Lieutenant+
Traffic Stop — Training Guide
Staff Use Only
Training Officer Note
Conduct a live ride-along traffic stop with the cadet. Walk through each step verbally before letting them take the lead. Correct in real time — do not wait until after the stop.
Common Cadet Mistakes to Watch For
Activating lights before announcing on radio — must announce first.
Skipping Melonly CAD check — always run the vehicle before approaching.
Approaching from the passenger side — always driver's side unless unsafe.
Not noting passenger count and hands before making contact.
Attempting to conduct FST without GSP — must call for GSP assistance.
Forgetting to close the stop on radio and mark back in service.
Sign-Off Criteria
Cadet correctly announces on radio before lights.
Cadet runs Melonly CAD and verbally confirms results.
Cadet approaches correctly and makes professional contact.
Cadet issues correct outcome and logs in CAD.
Cadet closes the stop properly on radio.
Felony Stop — Training Guide
Staff Use Only
Training Officer Note
Use a controlled scenario for felony stop training — do not put a cadet in a live high-risk situation without prior walkthrough. Run a dry scenario first, then observe a live one before sign-off.
Scenario Setup (Dry Run)
1
Brief the cadet on the scenario: vehicle type, offense, and number of occupants. Do not reveal the outcome in advance.
2
Have the cadet verbally walk through every step before executing — from dispatch notification to CAD logging.
3
Pay close attention to VC command delivery. Commands must be loud, firm, and follow the approved script format.
4
Correct cover positioning — cadet must know to angle the vehicle and not advance on the suspect.
Common Mistakes
Advancing on the vehicle instead of directing suspects backward.
Searching the vehicle before all occupants are fully handcuffed and secured.
Weak or hesitant VC delivery — must project with authority.
Attempting to transport to any location other than the jail.
Pursuit — Training Guide
Staff Use Only
Important
Cadets may NOT initiate or lead a pursuit. They may ride as secondary only, under direct supervision of a Corporal or above. Do not allow a cadet to call out or make decisions during a live pursuit until cleared.
Training Sequence
1
Review the full pursuit policy verbally with the cadet. Confirm they understand the staff permission requirement and the priority timer before any live exposure.
2
Allow the cadet to ride as secondary in a supervised pursuit. Have them practice call-outs (direction, street, landmark) while the training officer confirms accuracy.
3
After the pursuit, debrief the cadet: were call-outs accurate? Did they maintain safe distance? Did they understand the PIT/spike chain of approval?
Call-Out Practice Format
Script
"Suspect [direction] on [street name], passing [landmark], approaching [next intersection or landmark]."

Example: "Suspect northbound on Thornton Road, passing Walmart, approaching Veterans Memorial Highway intersection."
Training Officer Notes
Staff Use Only
General Guidance
Training is a privilege for the cadet and a responsibility for the officer. Take it seriously — the quality of your training reflects on the whole department.
Correct mistakes in real time, not after the fact. Letting a mistake slide once teaches the cadet it's acceptable.
Never clear a cadet you're not confident in. If they're not ready, continue the ride-along. There is no rush.
Document any significant issues or repeated mistakes and report to the Lieutenant before sign-off.
Clearance must be formally granted by a Lieutenant or above — a training officer cannot unilaterally clear a cadet.
Clearance Sign-Off
Final Step
Once the cadet has completed all checklist items and demonstrated competency, submit a clearance recommendation to your Lieutenant. The Lieutenant will conduct a brief evaluation and issue formal clearance if satisfied.
