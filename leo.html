Traffic Stop Procedures
All Agencies
Initiation
1
Observe the violation and confirm it warrants a stop — speeding, equipment violations, erratic driving, suspicious plates, etc.
2
Announce the stop over radio before activating lights. Provide vehicle description, color, direction of travel, and your unit number.
Example: "Unit 12, traffic stop on a red sedan northbound on Thornton Road, requesting Melonly CAD."
3
Activate emergency lights. If the vehicle does not yield, use a brief siren burst before escalating further.
Approach & Contact
4
Once stopped, run the vehicle through Melonly CAD. Gather the driver's name and check for warrants, registration, and license status.
5
Approach from the driver's side. Note passengers, visible hands, and any suspicious movement before making contact.
6
Greet the driver, state the reason for the stop, and request license, registration, and proof of insurance.
Field Sobriety Testing (FST)
GSP Only
GSP is the only agency authorized to conduct FSTs. All other agencies must request GSP assistance if impairment is suspected.
7
If impairment is suspected, GSP may order the driver to exit and administer a standard FST — HGN, walk-and-turn, and one-leg stand.
8
If the FST is failed, place the driver under arrest for DUI. Notify dispatch and document in Melonly CAD immediately.
Conclusion
9
Issue a citation, verbal warning, or make an arrest based on findings. Log all outcomes in Melonly CAD.
10
Notify dispatch the stop is complete and you are back in service.
Felony Stop Procedures
High Risk
When to Use
Initiate a felony stop when a vehicle is confirmed or reasonably suspected to be involved in a felony — robbery, weapons offense, active felony warrant, etc.
1
Notify dispatch immediately — vehicle description, current location, and nature of the felony. Request backup before initiating.
2
Maintain a safe following distance. Do not crowd the vehicle. Wait for backup when possible before making contact.
3
Activate lights and siren. Deliver commands clearly over VC, projecting firmly as if over a PA system:
"This is [your agency]. Driver — turn off the engine and throw your keys out of the window now."
"All occupants — exit the vehicle one at a time, hands raised above your head. Walk slowly backward toward the sound of my voice."
"Do not reach for anything. Do not make any sudden movements. Failure to comply will be met with force."
4
Position vehicles at an angle for cover. Draw service weapon. Do not advance — direct suspects to walk backward to your position.
5
Handcuff and detain all occupants before clearing the vehicle. Search only after all occupants are fully secured.
6
Transport suspect(s) directly to the jail for booking. All bookings must be completed at the jail — no exceptions.
7
Log all details in Melonly CAD immediately, including any use-of-force incidents.
10-80 Pursuit Policy
Staff Permission Required
Staff Permission Required
All pursuits require staff permission before initiation. Permission is easily obtainable — this exists solely to prevent too many pursuits running simultaneously. Request permission via radio or VC from any available staff member before activating.
Priority Timer
Once permission is granted, a priority timer must be started immediately. The pursuit is authorized only for the duration of that active timer. If the timer expires, reassess continuation with staff before proceeding.
Conduct During Pursuit
Announce the pursuit immediately — state direction, road name, and full vehicle description.
Secondary units are responsible for continuous call-outs. Include direction, street name, and nearby landmarks at every turn.
Example: "Suspect northbound on Thornton Road, passing Walmart, approaching Veterans Memorial Highway intersection."
Only GSP units are authorized to perform a PIT maneuver. All other agencies are prohibited from PITing under any circumstance.
Permission to PIT must be explicitly granted by a supervisor before execution. Announce intent on radio and wait for verbal confirmation.
Spike strips are for high-priority chases only and must be approved by a Captain or above. Deploy at a pre-announced location — avoid intersections, hills, bends, or high civilian traffic areas.
No Grapplers are authorized under any circumstances.
LEO vehicle count must remain realistic. Do not flood the pursuit — only authorized responding units may be involved.
Moderation Infraction
Driving on the opposite lane of traffic is strictly prohibited at all times, including during active pursuits. Violations will result in a moderation infraction with no exceptions.
Arrest Procedures
All Agencies
1
Confirm you have probable cause or an active warrant before placing anyone under arrest. Do not arrest without justification.
2
Announce the arrest clearly — inform the suspect they are under arrest and state the specific charge(s).
3
Handcuff the suspect. Conduct a pat-down search for weapons and contraband before transport. Do not skip this step.
4
Read Miranda rights before any questioning begins. If the suspect invokes their right to remain silent, all questioning must cease immediately.
5
Transport the suspect directly to the jail. All bookings must be completed at the jail — suspects may not be processed at any other location.
6
Notify dispatch of transport and ETA. Complete the full arrest report in Melonly CAD including charges, evidence, and any use-of-force incidents.

Uniform SOP
All Agencies
Policy
All officers must wear the uniform class assigned to their current duty type. Wearing the incorrect class while on duty is a uniform violation.
Class A
Formal Dress
Required for training events, formal ceremonies, and official department functions.
Class B
Standard Patrol
Basic patrol uniform — long sleeve. Worn during standard patrol shifts.
Class C
Daily Patrol
Short sleeve uniform for daily patrol and general duty.
