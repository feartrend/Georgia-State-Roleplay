Policy April 7, 2025 Pinned
ALPR (License Plate Reader) — New Policy, Effective Immediately
Automatic License Plate Readers (ALPR) are now authorized for use under the following conditions, effective immediately:

Douglasville Police Department (DVPD): ALPR use is permitted on the PRANCERS ONLY without any additional authorization. All active DVPD officers may use ALPR freely during patrol. No permission is required.

Georgia State Patrol (GSP): ALPR use requires special authorization. GSP personnel must obtain permission from a Lieutenant or above before deploying ALPR. Unauthorized use by GSP will be treated as a policy violation.

Cobb County Sheriff's Office: ALPR is not authorized for Cobb County units at this time under any circumstances.

These permissions are subject to change. Any updates will be posted here.
Posted by GSR Staff Team
Policy March 28, 2025 Pinned
SOP Launch — All Officers Must Review
The official Georgia State Roleplay Standard Operating Procedures are now live. All active LEO personnel across GSP, DVPD, and Cobb County are required to review the full SOP before their next patrol shift. Key areas to focus on: pursuit permission requirements, the priority timer system, felony stop VC command format, and the jail-only booking policy.

Failure to follow updated SOPs may result in in-game consequences or moderation action. If you have questions, reach out to a Lieutenant or above in your respective agency.
Posted by GSR Staff Team
Update March 25, 2025
GSP Chain of Command Updated
The GSP chain of command has been revised to more closely reflect the real Georgia State Patrol rank structure. The top command now runs Colonel → Lieutenant Colonel, with full trooper tiers added at the patrol level (Master Trooper, Trooper First Class, Trooper, Probationary Trooper). All GSP personnel should familiarize themselves with the updated structure on the LEO page.
Posted by GSR Staff Team
